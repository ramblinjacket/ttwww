üöÄ Getting Started

Quick Start

Get scoring in 5 easy steps:

  1. Open Toss Tally and you'll land on the Setup tab
  2. Enter team names by tapping the text fields
  3. Tap "Start New Match" at the bottom of the screen
  4. Select a scoring mode (Tap to Score for quick games, Bag Scoring for detailed tracking)
  5. Start scoring ‚Äî swipe to the Scorekeeping tab and record each round

App Overview

Toss Tally has three main tabs you can swipe between:

Tab Icon Purpose
Setup Gear Configure teams, players, match format, and broadcasting
Scorekeeping Center Record scores during gameplay
Feed Activity Watch nearby matches and view statistics

Screen Navigation

  • Swipe left or right to move between tabs
  • The current tab is indicated by the position of the view
  • During your first use, on-screen hints will guide you through swiping

Before starting a game, configure your match settings on the Setup tab.

Team Names and Colors

To change a team name:

  • Tap the team name text field
  • Type the new name
  • Tap elsewhere or press return to save

To change a team color:

  • Tap the colored circle next to the team name
  • Select a new color from the picker

To swap team positions:

Adding Players

For doubles play, you can add up to 2 players per team:

  1. Tap the player section for a team

  3. Enter the player's name
  4. Repeat for additional players

Managing your player directory:

  • Swipe left on a player name to delete them
  • Tap a player name to edit it
  • Players are saved across all future matches

Scoring Mode Selection

When you tap Start New Match, you'll choose a scoring mode:

  • Tap to Score ‚Äî Quick and simple, best for casual games
  • Bag Scoring ‚Äî Detailed tracking with statistics
Note: Once a match starts, the scoring mode cannot be changed. Start a new match to switch modes.

üéØ Scoring Modes

Toss Tally offers two main scoring approaches, each suited to different play styles.

Tap to Score

Best for: Casual games, quick scoring, when you don't need detailed statistics

How it works:

  • The screen rotates to landscape orientation
  • Each team has a large score panel
  • Tap the top half of a panel to add points (+1)
  • Tap the bottom half to subtract points (-1)
  • Scores update instantly with visual feedback

Features:

  • Large, easy-to-read score display
  • Quick tap gestures for fast-paced games
  • Game counter shows progress in multi-game matches
  • Winner announcement when a team reaches 21

Bag Scoring

Best for: Competitive play, tracking player statistics, detailed game analysis

How it works:

  • The screen stays in portrait orientation
  • Record each bag's landing individually
  • Statistics are calculated automatically

Track All Bags

Record every bag thrown:

  • Tap In for bags through the hole (3 points)
  • Tap On for bags on the board (1 point)
  • Tap Off for missed bags (0 points)

This provides the most accurate statistics but takes slightly longer to enter.

Skip Off Bags

Only record successful throws:

  • Tap In for bags through the hole
  • Tap On for bags on the board
  • Off bags are calculated automatically (4 minus In minus On)

This speeds up scoring while maintaining accuracy.

Enter Totals

Enter the point total directly:

  • Select points scored (0 to 12)
  • The app determines the bag breakdown

For some point values, multiple breakdowns are possible:

  • 3 points could be 1 In + 3 Off, or 3 On + 1 Off
  • 4 points could be 1 In + 1 On + 2 Off, or 4 On
  • 6 points could be 2 In + 2 Off, or 1 In + 3 On

When this happens, a popup asks you to select the correct breakdown.

üìù Keeping Score

Using Tap to Score

  1. After starting a match, swipe to the Scorekeeping tab
  2. The view rotates to landscape automatically
  3. Tap the top half of a team's panel to add 1 point
  4. Tap the bottom half to subtract 1 point (for corrections)
  5. Continue until a team reaches 21 points

The score displays update with a subtle animation when changed.

Using Bag Scoring

  1. After starting a match, swipe to the Scorekeeping tab
  2. You'll see both teams' scoring panels
  3. For each team, record bags using the In/On/Off buttons:
    • Each button shows a count and point total
    • Tap to increment the count
    • The button updates to show bags recorded
  4. Each team can record up to 4 bags per round

Submitting Rounds

After both teams have recorded their bags:

  1. The Submit Round button becomes active
  2. Tap it to calculate and apply the round's scores
  3. Cancellation scoring is applied automatically
  4. The round counter advances
  5. Bag counts reset for the next round

Undoing Mistakes

Made an error? You can fix it:

Before submitting:

  • Tap a bag type button again to adjust the count
  • Counts cycle through valid values

After submitting:

  • Tap Revert Last Round to undo the submission
  • The previous round's data is restored
  • Make your corrections and resubmit

Game Over and Match Winner

When a team reaches 21 points:

  1. The game ends immediately
  2. A winner announcement appears
  3. Confetti celebrates the victory
  4. The game win is recorded for the match

In multi-game matches:

  • The win counter updates (shown as circles)
  • Tap to start the next game
  • When a team wins enough games, the match ends

üìã Cornhole Scoring Rules

Points System

Each bag earns points based on where it lands:

Landing Points Description
In3Bag goes through the hole
On1Bag lands and stays on the board
Off0Bag misses the board or slides off
Foul0Rule violation (stepping over the line, etc.)

Cancellation Scoring

Toss Tally uses cancellation scoring (also called "netting"), which is standard in competitive cornhole:

How it works:

  1. Both teams throw their 4 bags in a round
  2. Calculate each team's raw points (0-12 possible)
  3. Subtract the lower score from the higher score
  4. Only the team with more points scores ‚Äî they get the difference
Example:
Team A throws: 2 In (6 pts) + 1 On (1 pt) + 1 Off (0 pts) = 7 points
Team B throws: 1 In (3 pts) + 2 On (2 pts) + 1 Off (0 pts) = 5 points
Result: Team A scores 7 - 5 = 2 net points. Team B scores 0 points.

This keeps games competitive and prevents runaway scores.

Race to 21

  • The first team to reach 21 points wins the game
  • There is no "bust" rule ‚Äî you cannot go over and lose points
  • If both teams would reach 21 in the same round, the team with more points wins
  • Most games last 15-25 rounds

üì° Multiplayer Features

Share your game with spectators or collaborate with others to keep score using Bluetooth.

Broadcasting for Spectators

What it does: Shares your live scoreboard publicly so anyone nearby can watch.

To start broadcasting:

  1. On the Setup tab, find the "Broadcast" section
  2. Toggle on Broadcast for Spectators
  3. A status shows "Broadcasting" with viewer count

What spectators see:

  • Live score updates
  • Team names and colors
  • Current round and game information
  • They cannot modify scores ‚Äî view only

Range: Approximately 30-50 feet, depending on environment.

Hosting for Collaborators

What it does: Allows another person to help keep score from their own device.

To start hosting:

  1. On the Setup tab, find the "Host" section
  2. Toggle on Host for Collaborators
  3. A QR code appears on your screen
  4. Have your collaborator scan this code

Security:

  • The QR code contains a secure authentication key
  • Only people who scan the code can modify scores
  • Commands are cryptographically signed to prevent tampering

Dual Mode

Combine broadcasting and hosting:

  • Public spectators can watch (read-only)
  • Authenticated collaborators can help score
  • Ideal for tournaments or events with both audiences

Watching Nearby Matches

To find matches to watch:

  1. Go to the Feed tab
  2. The "Watch Nearby" section shows available matches
  3. Swipe through the carousel to browse matches
  4. Tap a match card to view the live scoreboard

What you'll see:

  • Team names and current scores
  • Signal strength indicator (colored bars)
  • "Stale" indicator if connection is lost

To disconnect: Tap the back button to return to the match list.

Joining as a Collaborator

To join someone's match as a collaborator:

  1. On the Setup tab, select Join mode
  2. Tap Scan QR Code
  3. Point your camera at the host's QR code
  4. Once connected, you can record scores

Your score changes:

  • Sync instantly with the host device
  • The host sees your updates in real-time
  • Both devices show the same scores

üí° Tips & Troubleshooting

Best Practices

For accurate statistics:

  • Enter player names before starting (consistent names track history)
  • Use Bag Scoring mode for detailed tracking
  • Submit rounds promptly to avoid forgetting throws

For smooth gameplay:

  • Charge your device before long sessions
  • Keep Bluetooth enabled for multiplayer features
  • Use Tap to Score for quick casual games

For multiplayer:

  • Stay within 30-50 feet of connected devices
  • Avoid physical obstructions between devices
  • Keep the app in the foreground while broadcasting

Bluetooth Troubleshooting

"Bluetooth must be enabled"

Open Settings > Bluetooth and turn it on. Return to Toss Tally.

"Bluetooth unauthorized"

Open Settings > Privacy > Bluetooth. Find Toss Tally and enable access. Restart the app.

Can't find nearby matches
  • Ensure Bluetooth is on for both devices
  • Move closer to the broadcasting device
  • Ask the host to verify they're broadcasting
  • Try pulling down to refresh the match list
Connection keeps dropping
  • Move closer to reduce interference
  • Check for obstacles (walls, crowds)
  • Verify both devices have sufficient battery
  • Try restarting the broadcast/connection

Common Questions

Can I change the scoring mode during a match?

No, scoring mode is locked once a match starts. Tap "Start New Match" to change modes, but this will end your current match.

Why does "Enter Totals" sometimes ask me to pick a breakdown?

Some point totals (3, 4, and 6) can result from different bag combinations. The app asks which one occurred so statistics remain accurate.

How far does Bluetooth reach?

Typically 30-50 feet in open space. Walls, crowds, and interference can reduce range.

Is my data synced to the cloud?

No, all data stays on your device. Use multiplayer features to share live scores with nearby devices.

Can I export my match history?

Match data is stored locally on your device. Currently, there is no export feature.

What happens if I close the app mid-game?

Your match is saved automatically. When you reopen the app, it resumes where you left off.
